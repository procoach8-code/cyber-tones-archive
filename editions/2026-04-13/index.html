AI & Automation

Anthropic Debuts Claude Mythos AI Model in New Cybersecurity Initiative Project Glasswing

Wired / TechCrunch · April 7, 2026

Anthropic has debuted a preview of its powerful new AI model, Claude Mythos, as part of a new cybersecurity initiative called Project Glasswing. The project brings together Apple, Google, and over 45 other organizations to use the Mythos model to test advancing AI cybersecurity capabilities. The initiative raises questions about how exploit-writing AI can be kept out of the wrong hands.

WHY IT MATTERS

AI models capable of writing exploits represent a double-edged sword — they can accelerate defensive security testing but also dramatically lower the bar for sophisticated attacks. CISOs need to begin policy frameworks for AI-assisted red teaming and consider how adversaries with access to similar models may change their threat landscape.

Security Bosses Are All in on AI: Here's Why

Dark Reading · April 2, 2026

A Dark Reading podcast featuring Reddit CISO Frederick Lee and Omdia analyst Dave Gruber reveals that security leaders are broadly embracing AI for security operations, with significant planned expansions of AI tooling. CISOs report AI is delivering measurable value in threat detection, alert triage, and analyst augmentation. The discussion also covers the future promise of agentic AI in SecOps workflows.

WHY IT MATTERS

This represents a significant shift in CISO sentiment — AI in SecOps is moving from experimental to operational. Security leaders who have not yet built an AI adoption roadmap for their security operations risk falling behind peers and missing efficiency gains that can address the ongoing talent shortage.

RSAC 2026: AI Dominates, But Community Remains Key to Security

Dark Reading · April 2, 2026

At RSAC 2026, AI dominated discussions across the security community while practitioners emphasized that human collaboration and community remain essential to effective cybersecurity. The conference highlighted the tension between AI-driven automation and the need for human judgment in complex security decisions. Multiple vendors showcased agentic AI capabilities for autonomous threat response.

WHY IT MATTERS

RSAC 2026 signals that agentic AI for security is moving from concept to product, with significant vendor investment. IT leaders should use this as a market signal to accelerate evaluation of AI-native SecOps platforms while ensuring human oversight frameworks are built into any autonomous response capabilities.

Cybersecurity Threats

France to ditch Windows for Linux to reduce reliance on US tech

TechCrunch · April 10, 2026

France has announced plans to migrate government systems from Windows to Linux in a bid to reduce reliance on US technology. The move reflects growing geopolitical concerns about dependency on American software vendors, particularly amid transatlantic tensions. This marks one of the most significant national-level OS migrations attempted by a major Western democracy.

WHY IT MATTERS

This signals a broader trend of sovereign tech strategies that IT leaders in government and regulated industries must monitor. CISOs should evaluate their own vendor concentration risks and consider how geopolitical shifts may impact software supply chains and licensing continuity.

Iran-Linked Hackers Disrupt Operations at US Critical Infrastructure Sites

Ars Technica / Wired · April 8, 2026

Iran-linked hackers have disrupted operations at multiple US critical infrastructure sites including energy and water facilities, according to US government warnings. The attacks have intensified as US-Israel military actions escalate, with nearly 4,000 internet-exposed industrial PLCs manufactured by Rockwell Automation identified as vulnerable. US agencies formally warned that Iranian threat actors are actively targeting American critical infrastructure.

WHY IT MATTERS

OT/ICS security is no longer a niche concern — geopolitical conflict is directly translating to kinetic-level disruption of industrial systems. Security leaders with any OT exposure must urgently audit internet-facing PLCs and ICS devices, segment OT networks, and review incident response plans for operational disruption scenarios.

Thousands of Consumer Routers Hacked by Russia's Military to Steal Credentials

Ars Technica · April 8, 2026

Russia's military intelligence agency (GRU) has hacked thousands of consumer and small-office routers across 120 countries to steal credentials and passwords. The campaign targeted end-of-life routers that no longer receive security patches. The operation underscores the persistent risk posed by unmanaged edge devices in both home and corporate environments.

WHY IT MATTERS

With remote work still prevalent, employee home routers remain a critical and often overlooked attack vector. IT leaders should establish policies around remote worker network security, push EOL device replacement programs, and consider zero-trust architectures that do not rely on network perimeter integrity.
